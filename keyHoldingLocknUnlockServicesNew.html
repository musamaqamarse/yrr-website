KEY HOLDING LOCK AND UNLOCK SERVICES

Security is a critical aspect of managing any business, and the lock and unlock services provided by Year-Round Security offer a valuable solution. By entrusting this responsibility to specialized security personnel, businesses can ensure that their premises are protected effectively. This service not only enhances security but also provides convenience and reliability, allowing business owners to focus on their core operations without worrying about the safety of their property after hours.

WE’LL LOCK AND UNLOCK YOUR BUSINESS FOR YOU

Many businesses will allocate an internal key holder, responsible for locking and unlocking your business at the start and end of the day. Whilst this can work for some businesses, placing this responsibility on one employee can sometimes cause disruption to your business. Be sure there’s always a trusted and reliable security and/patrol officer to open and close your business for you. With our highly trained security guards, enjoy the peace of mind that your employees will never be a target for criminals looking to access your property.

THE SECURITY MEASURES YEAR-ROUND SECURITY TAKES:


  • Risk Assessment: Conducting thorough risk assessments to identify potential security threats and vulnerabilities specific to the vacant property.
  • Security Personnel: Employing static or on-site security personnel who are highly trained and experienced in property protection.
  • Strategic Positioning: Placing security professionals in strategic locations to monitor and manage the premises effectively.
  • Deterrence Measures: Implementing robust deterrence measures to prevent unauthorized access and discourage potential intruders.
  • Incident Response: Ensuring a swift and effective response to any disturbances, minimizing risks and damage to the property.
  • Access Control: Establishing strict access control protocols to manage entry and exit points, ensuring only authorized individuals gain access.
  • Regular Patrols: Conducting regular patrols around the property to detect and respond to any irregular activities.
  • Maintenance Checks: Performing routine maintenance checks to ensure security equipment and infrastructure are functioning optimally.
  • Stakeholder Communication: Maintaining clear and regular communication with property stakeholders regarding security status and incident reports.
  • Training and Development: Providing ongoing training and development for security personnel to keep them updated on the latest security practices and technologies.
  • Legal Compliance: Ensuring all security measures comply with local laws and regulations to avoid any legal repercussions.
  • Emergency Preparedness: Developing and rehearsing emergency procedures for potential scenarios such as fire, break-ins, or natural disasters.
  • Collaboration with Law Enforcement: Establishing a good working relationship with local law enforcement agencies for additional support when necessary.
  • Insurance Considerations: Reviewing insurance policies to ensure adequate coverage for the property and liability protection for security actions.
  • Community Engagement: Engaging with the local community to foster a collaborative approach to security and property protection.
